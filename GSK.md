    ### Reflection: Patient Focus
    I often reflect on the gap between a technical design and the person using it.
    During the Epitrainer project, I realised that user confidence is as vital as the device itself.
    I worked with medical professionals to understand their daily reality and address their pain points.
    I learned that empathy is a technical necessity. This user-centric focus is a principle I hope to apply to patient needs at GSK.
    I believe that engineering should always serve the individual first.


    ### Reflection: Digital Technology (SWE)
    I reflect on software engineering as a bridge between complex data and human impact.
    At WMG, I used React and TypeScript to build robust interfaces for intelligent vehicles.
    I see how GSK uses machine learning to transform drug discovery.
    This requires a developer to simplify complexity rather than add to it.
    I have used Agile methods to maintain high-impact goals during intense projects.
    Technical rigour is a form of accountability for the reliability of systems that support human health.


    ### Reflection: UX Implementation
    I approach UX design as an iterative process of validation.
    At GSK, I will implement this by running Design Sprints to move quickly from research to interactive prototypes.
    I will conduct patient engagement research to create accurate personas and journey maps.
    My experience with Figma will help me apply the GSK Digital Design System for consistent user experiences.
    I will prioritise accessibility to ensure that medical information is clear for all users.
    I believe that inclusive design leads to better health outcomes.
